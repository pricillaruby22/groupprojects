Gulab Jamun

  1. Make a dough from khoya and flour.
  2. Shape into balls and fry until golden.
  3. Soak in sugar syrup flavored with rose water.

Jalebi

  1. Mix flour, yogurt, and water to make a batter.
  2. Pipe in circular shapes and fry until golden.
  3. Soak in warm sugar syrup.

Kheer

  1. Boil milk and add soaked rice or vermicelli.
  2. Cook until thick, add sugar, and cardamom.
  3. Garnish with nuts and serve warm or cold.

Rasgulla

  1. Boil milk, curdle with lemon juice, and strain.
  2. Knead chenna until smooth, shape into balls.
  3. Cook in sugar syrup until they double in size.

Barfi

  1. Cook khoya with sugar and cardamom until thick.
  2. Spread in a greased plate, let cool.
  3. Cut into pieces and garnish with nuts.

Ladoo

  1. Roast flour or semolina with ghee.
  2. Add sugar, nuts, and cardamom.
  3. Shape into balls while warm.

Peda

  1. Cook khoya with sugar and cardamom.
  2. Cool slightly, then shape into small discs.
  3. Garnish with nuts and serve.

Chum Chum

  1. Make chenna and shape into oval pieces.
  2. Boil in sugar syrup until they double in size.
  3. Cool and serve with coconut or khoya topping.

Sooji Halwa

  1. Roast semolina in ghee until golden.
  2. Add water and sugar, stir until thick.
  3. Garnish with nuts and serve warm.

Kesar Pista Kulfi

  1. Boil milk and reduce it to half.
  2. Add sugar, saffron, and crushed pistachios.
  3. Pour into molds and freeze until set.

Rava Kesari

  1. Roast semolina in ghee.
  2. Add water, sugar, and color, stirring continuously.
  3. Garnish with nuts and serve warm.

Doodh Peda

  1. Reduce milk to a thick consistency.
  2. Add sugar, cardamom, and shape into discs.
  3. Garnish with nuts and serve.

Mysore Pak

  1. Heat ghee and add besan, stirring continuously.
  2. Add sugar syrup and cook until thick.
  3. Spread in a plate, cool, and cut into pieces.
